Antares




The antares rocket was developed by orbitalATK(now owned by northrop grunman) to supply cargo to the iss under NASA's Commercial Orbital
Transportation Services (COTS) program.

Originally the antares rocket wasnt able to get the COTS contract.It was only after Rocketplane Kistler failed to acquire funding they got selected.
The antares was originally called the Taurus 2.It is used to launch the cygnus cargo spacecraft to the iss.

Antares 100 series

The AJ26 which is a modified form of NK-33 which is a closed cycle ox-rich kerolox engine was used to power the first stage.43 of which were purchased
by Aerojet in the 1990s. Twenty of these were refurbished into AJ26 engines for Antares.Modifications included equipping the engines for gimballing,
adding US electronics, and qualifying the engines to fire for twice as long as designed and to operate at 108% of their original thrust.Following the
catastrophic failure of an AJ26 during testing at Stennis Space Center in May 2014 and the Orb-3 launch failure in October 2014,the 100 series was retired.

Antares 200 series

Because of concerns over corrosion, aging, and the limited supply of AJ26 engines,the Russian RD-181(modified form of the soviet RD-191) was used.The
Antares 200 and 200+ first stages are powered by two RD-181 engines, which provide 440 kilonewtons more thrust than the dual AJ26 engines used
on the Antares 100. 200 series adapted the originally ordered 100 Series stages,but it required under-throttling the RD181 which reduced performance. The antares 230+ had structural changes made to the intertank bay (between the LO2 and RP-1 tanks) and the forward bay (forward of the LO2).


The second stage is an Orbital ATK Castor 30-series solid-fuel rocket.The first two flights of Antares used a Castor 30A, which was replaced by the
enhanced Castor 30B which had thrust vector control capability.Antares offers three optional third stages: the Bi-Propellant Third Stage (BTS), a Star
48-based third stage and an Orion 38 motor.

Cygnus spacecraft-

Missions-

Antares A-ONE -
Antares A-ONE was the maiden flight of the antares rocket.It carried a cygnus mass simulator(fake cygnus) to orbit along with four cubesats.

CRS Orb-3-
On October 28, 2014, an Antares carrying a Cygnus cargo spacecraft on the Orb-3 resupply mission failed catastrophically six seconds after
liftoff.The thrust section exploded,cauing the rocket to fall back to the pad and explode.It was caused by a failure failure of the first
stage LOX turbopump.This delayed future antares missions,and cygnus spacecrafts were launched on an atlas 5 instead(OA-4,Oa-6).
