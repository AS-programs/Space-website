Delta 2-

Delta 2 was originally built by McDonell Douglas company which was later merged with boeing in 1997.The first
stage of a delta 2 had rocketdyne RS-27 engine and was called as 'Extra-Extended Long Tank Thor' sine it was a
derivative of the thor ballistic missile.Delta 2 also had SRB's.For the 6000-series, Delta II used Castor 4A
boosters, while the 7000-series used Graphite-Epoxy Motors manufactured by ATK.The second stage had a Aerojet
AJ10 engine.There was a third stage present for beyond LEO destinations such as GTO or trans-Mars trajectory.
This stage was powered by solid fuel and was spin stabilized,meaning it spinned during stage separtion to give
the satellite more stability.After stage sep to reduce the spins the satellite used a yo-yo de spin mechanism.
The satellite released long threads with weights at the end.To conserve angular momentum the satellite slowed
down.Then the threads were ejected.
The delta 2 used a 4 digit naming system-
The first digit was either 6 or 7, denoting the 6000- or 7000-series Delta.
The second digit indicated the number of boosters. Most Delta II rockets flew with 9 boosters, but some flew with
3 or 4.
The third digit was always 2, denoting a second stage with an Aerojet AJ10 engine. Only Deltas prior to the
6000-series used a different engine, the TR-201.
The last digit denoted the third stage. 0 denoted no third stage, 5 indicated a Payload Assist Module (PAM)
stage with Star 48B solid motor, and 6 indicated a Star 37FM motor.
An H following the four digits denoted that the vehicle used the larger Delta III GEM-46 boosters.

Delta 3

Delta 3 or the delta 8930 was the next delta rocket.Unlike delta 2,it used kerosene and oxygen.It had 2 vernier engines for controlling roll.The diameter of
the kerosene tank was increased from 2.4 meters to 4 meters.This reduced the overall length of the vehicle and allowed the Delta III to use the same launch
facilities as the Delta II with only minor modifications.First stage had 9 GEM 46 solid rocket boosters.Six were ignited on the launch pad, while the
remaining three were ignited just before burnout and separation of the ground-lit boosters. To maintain steering authority, three of the boosters had
vectoring nozzles.The delta 3 had a DCSS(Delta Cryogenic Upper Stage) which burnt liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen.The RL-10B-2 was similar to
RL-10 but had electromechanical actuators for gimbal control and extended nozzle for highrt specific impulse.Extended nozzle increases specific impulse
as less thrust is wasted in gas expanding outwards.The rocket had only 3 launches.2 failed and one was a partial failure reaching lower than planned
orbit.
