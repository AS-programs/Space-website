Soyuz

Space Centre in Samara,Russia.Soyuz is the most frequaently used launch vehicle with over 1900 flights since its debut in 1966.

Soyuz(rocket) -

The soyuz is a soviet expendable launch vehicle designed to launch crew aboard the soyuz spacecraft.It launched the spacecraft 27 times(8 times uncrewed)
and 19 crewed).Its first four test launches were all failures, but eventually it worked.The aborted Soyuz 18-1 launch in 1975 was the final crewed flight of
this rocket as it was going to be replaced by the Soyuz-U.

Soyuz-L

The soyuz was created to test the LK lunar lander in LEO.The Soyuz-L was a derivative of the original Soyuz rocket featuring
the reinforced first stage and boosters supporting the Molniya-M's third stage, so that it could carry a more massive payload.
A larger payload fairing was also fitted, to accommodate the LK spacecraft.

Soyuz-U -

The soyuz-U was an improved version of the original soyuz rocket.It was one of the most reliable launch vehicles with
a world record 786 missions and a success rate of 97.3%.Two versions of Soyuz-U with different upper stages were the
Soyuz-U/Ikar and the Soyuz-U/Fregat.The performance of the Ikar upper stage is lower than that of the Fregat upper stage,
but it is more precise in maneuvering and it can operate autonomously longer.

Soyuz-M

The aoyuz-M is another version of soyuz built to launch military soyuz spacecraft.But after the cancellation of that
programme,development of the rocket was abandoned and the rockets that were completed were used to launch reconnaissance
satellites.

Soyuz-U2 -

The Soyuz-U2 was a Soviet, later Russian, carrier rocket.It had increased performance compared to the soyuz U2 due to
the use of syntin propellant,as opposed to RP-1 parrafin used on soyuz U2.The increased payload of the Soyuz-U2 allowed
heavier spacecraft to be launched, while lighter spacecraft could be placed in higher orbits.In 1996, it was announced
that the Soyuz-U2 had been retired, as the performance advantage gained through the use of syntin did not justify the
additional cost of its production.It was used to launch crewed soyuz missions,progress spacecraft crgo missions and
reconnaissance satellites.

Soyuz-FG

The Soyuz-FG launch vehicle was an improved version of the Soyuz-U.Soyuz-FG made its maiden flight on 20 May 2001,
carrying a Progress cargo spacecraft to the International Space Station (ISS).Its first failure occured due to a faulty
sensor on the MS-10 mission.It was retired after the 25 September 2019 launch of Soyuz MS-15 to the ISS; the analog control
system significantly limited its capabilities and prompted its replacement by Soyuz-2.

Soyuz-2

Soyuz-2 is a modernized version of the Soviet Soyuz rocket.It has engines with better injection system.It also has digital
flight control and telementery systems which allow the rocket to be launch from a fixed platfrom whereas the launch platforms
for earlier Soyuz rockets had to be rotated as the rocket could not perform a roll to change its heading in flight.The Soyuz-2
family includes 2.1a/STA, 2.1b/STB, and 2.1v.The 2.1b version adds an upgraded engine (RD-0124) which greatly increases the
specific impulse of the upper stage (326 seconds to 359 seconds), and hence improves payload capability from 7 tonnes to 8.2
tonnes.It is a "light" version of the Soyuz-2 without the side boosters (blocks B, V, G and D).2.1v can veliver up to 2.8 tonnes
to low Earth orbit.


One thing common among all soyuz rockets is that they an open trusswork in between stages.Why is this so?

It is for 'hot staging'.Here the second stage ingnites before the stage sep.This is done to accelarate the second stage to bring the fuel down
into the engine.Usually for this ullage motors are used which fire after stage separation.The disadvantage of doing hot staging is that it wastes
some fuel.The open trusswork ensure there is no pressure buildup in the interstage.
